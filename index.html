DR. A.P.J ABDUL KALAM

1931 - 2015

Missile Man Of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was a great scientist, a visionary leader, and a true patriot. He was known as the "Missile Man of India" for his contributions to the development of India's missile and space programs. He was also the 11th President of India. Dr. Kalam was born in a humble family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He studied physics and aerospace engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology, Madras. He then went on to work as a scientist at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Dr. Kalam was a brilliant scientist and engineer. He played a key role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, the SLV-III, and the Prithvi and Agni missiles. He was also the project director for the development of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV), which is India's workhorse launch vehicle. Dr. Kalam was not only a brilliant scientist, but also a wise and compassionate leader. He was elected as the 11th President of India in 2002. During his presidency, he focused on promoting science and technology education, and on improving the lives of the poor and the marginalized. Dr. Kalam was a true patriot and a role model for millions of Indians. He inspired people to dream big and to work hard to achieve their goals. He will be remembered as one of the greatest scientists and leaders of India. Here are some of his most famous quotes: "Man needs his difficulties because they are necessary to enjoy success." "Look at the sky. We are not alone. The whole universe is friendly to us and conspires only to give the best to those who dream and work." "If you want to shine like a sun, first burn like a sun." "You have to dream before your dreams can come true." "Let us sacrifice our today so that our children can have a better tomorrow." Dr. Kalam's legacy will live on for generations to come. He was a true inspiration to all who knew him, and his words will continue to motivate and guide us.

Biography

  • Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • Studied physics and aerospace engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology, Madras.
  • Joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in 1958.
  • Played a key role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, the SLV-III.
  • Joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1969.
  • Played a key role in the development of India's ballistic missile program, including the Prithvi and Agni missiles.
  • Project director for the development of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV), which is India's workhorse launch vehicle.
  • Appointed as the Scientific Adviser to the Defence Minister in 1992.
  • Appointed as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister in 1998.
  • Elected as the 11th President of India in 2002.
  • Served as President for a term of five years.
  • Died on July 27, 2015, at the age of 83.
